I. Problem Statement1. Problem DefinitionThe system currently lacks a financial risk gate at the refund execution stage, permitting merchants to initate refunds that exceed their pending settlement balances. This 'trust-first' model exposes the platform to uncollateralised merchant debt, particularly within the high-growth but high-volatility SME and SOB segmentThe system currently applies a net-off settlement formula, where refunds are deducted from merchant revenue during T+1 settlement. However, refunds can still be executed before sufficient revenue exists.Refund request is initiated by merchantRefund Core processes the requestSystem triggers fund-out to the userMerchant balance becomes negative until settlementThe system does not perform a pre-check on merchant revenue sufficiency before allowing refunds.2. Problem ImpactFinancial RiskRefunds executed without sufficient merchant revenue can create merchant debt toward the platform.If the merchant ceases operations, becomes inactive or defaults, the platform absorbs the financial loss.Operational OverheadThe absence of refund controls creates, manual reconciliation workload, manual merchant debt recovery, and operational investigation for negative balancesThese activities create ongoing operational burden for Finance and Operations teams.3. Desired OutcomeThe system should enforce refund risk control mechanisms that ensure:Refunds cannot create negative merchant balances unless explicitly allowed.High-risk merchants cannot initiate refunds without sufficient revenue.Refund validation occurs before fund-out execution.4. Problem UrgencyIf no action is taken:financial loss risk increasesoperational recovery costs increasetrust in settlement accuracy deterioratesGiven the platform’s transaction scale, this risk must be addressed before further merchant expansion.II.
